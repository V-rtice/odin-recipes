Peruvian Roasted Chicken With Yellow Potatoes (Pollo a la Brasa)

Description

Pollo a la brasa is Peruvian-style rotisserie chicken that has become quite popular in the US,
thanks to its unique seasoning and the delicious sauces and side dishes that accompany it.

Ingredient List

  • 1 whole chicken
  • 3 pounds of yellow potatoes
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup vinegar
  • 3 cloves garlic
  • 1 teaspoon chili powder
  • 1 teaspoon cumin
  • 1 teaspoon garlic salt
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 tablespoon soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon fresh oregano, chopped
  • Optional : 1 tablespoon fresh mint

Steps to Make It

  1. Gather the ingredients.
  2. Brine the chicken (optional) Soak the chicken (giblets removed) in salt water for 12 hours in the refrigerator (brining poultry).
    Remove from salt water and rinse.
  3. Peel and mash the garlic, and mix it with the vinegar and the vegetable oil.
    Stir in the chili powder, cumin, paprika, garlic salt, soy sauce, oregano, and mint.
  4. Place the chicken in a ziplock bag, and pour in the marinade. Seal the back and toss to coat the chicken with the marinade.
  5. Marinate chicken, refrigerated, for 12 to 24 hours.
  6. Preheat oven to 375 F.
  7. Peel the potatoes and cut into small wedges.
  8. Place the potatoes in a roasting pan. Place the chicken, breast-side up, on top of a rack over the potatoes.
  9. Roast chicken uncovered for 30 to 45 minutes. If the skin is getting too brown, cover loosely with foil. Flip and stir the potatoes with a spatula.
    Continue to roast until chicken has an internal temperature of about 165 F, about 1 1/2 hours total oven time, depending on the size of the chicken. (Read more about how to roast chicken).
  10. Remove chicken from oven and let cool for 10 to 15 minutes before carving.
  11. Serve and enjoy!
